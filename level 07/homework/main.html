Top 5 Highest-Grossing Animated
Movies

1. Avatar

Gross Revenue:$2.923 billion

Release Date: December 18, 2009

Studio: 20th Century Fox

Story:

In the year 2154, humans are mining for a valuable mineral called unobtanium on the lush alien moon of Pandora. Jake Sully, a paraplegic former Marine, is chosen to participate in the Avatar Program, which allows humans to control genetically engineered avatars that resemble the indigenous Na'vi. As Jake explores Pandora and its culture, he forms a deep bond with Neytiri, a Na'vi princess. Conflicted between his loyalty to the humans and his love for Neytiri and Pandora, Jake ultimately leads the Na'vi in a battle against human exploitation.

2. Frozen II

Gross Revenue: $1.450 billion

Release Date: November 22, 2019

Studio: Walt Disney Animation Studios

Story:

Elsa hears a mysterious voice calling to her, prompting her to seek the truth about her powers and the history of Arendelle. Along with her sister Anna, Kristoff, Olaf, and Sven, Elsa journeys to an enchanted forest that has been trapped in mist for years. There, they uncover secrets about their family's past, the origins of Elsa's magic, and the need to restore balance to their kingdom. The film explores themes of self-discovery, family bonds, and confronting the past.

3. Frozen

Gross Revenue: $1.290 billion

Release Date: November 27, 2013

Studio: Walt Disney Animation Studios

Story:

Princess Elsa of Arendelle possesses magical powers that allow her to create ice and snow. After accidentally revealing her abilities during her coronation, she flees to the mountains, plunging Arendelle into eternal winter. Her younger sister, Anna, embarks on a quest to find Elsa and bring her back. Along the way, Anna teams up with an ice seller named Kristoff, his loyal reindeer Sven, and the whimsical snowman Olaf. Together, they face challenges and discover the power of love and sacrifice, ultimately teaching Elsa to embrace her true self.

4. Incredibles 2

Gross Revenue: $1.243 billion

Release Date: June 15, 2018

Studio: Pixar Animation Studios

Story:

Following the events of the first film, the superhero family—Bob (Mr. Incredible), Helen (Elastigirl), and their kids—struggle to live normal lives as superheroes are still illegal. When a new villain, Screenslaver, emerges, Helen is recruited to help bring superheroes back into the spotlight, while Bob stays home to manage their kids. As they face challenges in their roles, both parents learn the importance of teamwork and supporting each other's strengths, culminating in a thrilling showdown against Screenslaver.

5.Minions

Gross Revenue: $1.159 billion

Release Date: July 10, 2015

Studio: Illumination Entertainment

Story:

The film explores the history of the Minions, small yellow creatures who seek to serve the most despicable master they can find. After failing to serve various villains throughout history, they find themselves without a master. The story follows Kevin, Stuart, and Bob as they embark on a quest to find a new villain to serve, leading them to a convention for supervillains where they meet the ambitious Scarlet Overkill. Their misadventures highlight their loyalty, silliness, and desire for purpose.
